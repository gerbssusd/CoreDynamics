# The Mechanics of Improvement

<div class="grid grid-cols-2 gap-8">
<div>
  <h3 class="text-primary">Principle 1</h3>
  <p>Increases in student learning occur <b>only</b> as a consequence of improvements in:</p>
  <ul>
    <li>Teacher's Knowledge</li>
    <li>Student Engagement</li>
    <li>Level of Content</li>
  </ul>
</div>
<div>
  <h3 class="text-primary">Principle 2</h3>
  <p>Change to one element triggers change to the remaining two elements.</p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="mt-8 p-4 bg-gray-100 rounded">
  "You cannot raise the rigor of the content without simultaneously stretching teacher knowledge and student engagement. The system self-balances."
</div>

# The Sequence of Observation

### Principle 7: Description before analysis. Analysis before prediction. Prediction before evaluation.

1. **Step 1: Description**
2. **Step 2: Analysis**
3. **Step 3: Prediction**
4. **Step 4: Evaluation**

> "The most common diagnostic failure is skipping the lower steps and leaping directly from description to evaluation."

---

# The Integrated Protocol
### Applying the Seven Principles in the Classroom

* **Step 1. Describe:** What is the physical task in front of the student?
* **Step 2. Analyze:** How are the Teacher, Student, and Content interacting right now?
* **Step 3. Predict:** Based only on this task, what performance can we predict?
* **Step 4. Evaluate:** Does this align with our true accountability system?
